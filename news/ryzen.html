AMD to Launch Ryzen 7 9800X3D Processor Sooner Than Expected

Following underwhelming sales of Ryzen 9000 processors and the imminent launch of Intel's next-gen processors, rumors suggest that AMD is hurrying to release one of the Ryzen 9000X3D processors. One of the most anticipated processors this year is the Ryzen 7 9800X3D, set to replace the popular Ryzen 7 7800X3D in the new Ryzen 9000 series.

This processor, which leverages Zen5 microarchitecture and 3D V-cache technology, is expected to be a top choice for gamers due to its superior performance compared to the previous version. According to reports, AMD plans to release this processor by late October 2024, boasting a 15-20% performance increase over the Ryzen 7 9700X and aimed at competing with Intel's Core Ultra 200 processors.

Initially, rumors suggested that the Ryzen 9000X3D processors would be delayed until CES 2025, but recent news indicates we may see at least one processor from the series launched this year. Based on leaked information from an AMD employee at the X870 and X870E motherboard launch event, the Ryzen 7 9800X3D is expected to be officially revealed in September and launched in October.

While there are no official details on the processor's specs, it is rumored to feature 8 cores and 16 threads, along with 96MB of L3 cache and 8MB of L2 cache. If the release date is accurate, it will be the only processor with 3D V-cache and Zen5 technology available this year. We may need to wait longer for other variants, such as the Ryzen 9 9950X3D and Ryzen 9 9900X3D.
