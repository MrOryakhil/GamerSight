39% of Active Web Pages Have Disappeared Since 2013: A Digital Void is Growing

Recent reports have surfaced revealing a terrifying situation regarding web pages, showing that a large portion of the web is disappearing, creating a massive void on the internet. According to the report, around 39% of active web pages since 2013 have vanished. Learn more on GamerSight.

Many users believe that the internet can serve as an eternal archive of human knowledge, but it seems things are not going as planned. The report indicates that 39% of all active web pages since 2013 have disappeared, and even newer pages are vanishing rapidly.

The Threat of Content Disappearance Looms Over the Internet

This report, published by the Pew Internet & American Life Project, highlights the existence of a nonprofit organization called Internet Archive, which aims to preserve our online past.

Founded in 1996, Internet Archive is one of humanityâ€™s most important efforts to preserve digital heritage. This organization serves as a comprehensive digital library, storing web content. To date, it has archived around 866 billion web pages, 44 million books, and 10.6 million videos.

Internet Archive also offers a tool called Wayback Machine, which allows users to view deleted or altered website content. However, this internet time machine also faces new legal and technical challenges.

Wikipedia, one of the largest databases, now faces significant issues with "link rot" or decaying links. This means that the links to the pages cited by Wikipedia no longer exist.

According to a report by ecoticias, the problem of disappearing internet content has also spread to social networks. For example, about 15% of tweets are deleted within a few months after being posted. In any case, the internet is currently facing an issue that could affect its future, pushing many organizations to consider solutions to tackle this problem.
