NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5090 and RTX 5080 Leaked Specs: Massive Performance Gap

Thanks to a well-known hardware leaker on Twitter, new information has emerged about the final versions of NVIDIA's upcoming GeForce RTX 50 series graphics cards. The two leaked cards, the GeForce RTX 5090 and RTX 5080, show a significant gap in performance and specifications, more so than ever before.

GeForce RTX 5090 vs RTX 5080: Key Specifications

According to renowned NVIDIA insider kopite7Kimi, the RTX 5090 will feature 21,760 CUDA cores and 32GB of GDDR7 memory, with a 512-bit bus offering a bandwidth of 1,568GB/s. This flagship card will operate at a power consumption of 600W.

In comparison, the GeForce RTX 5080 will have 10,752 CUDA cores and 16GB of GDDR7 memory on a 256-bit bus, resulting in a bandwidth of 784GB/s, half that of the 5090. The 5080 will operate at a lower TDP of 400W.

Performance Gap and Insights

The RTX 5090 is expected to offer a 102% increase in CUDA cores over the RTX 5080, a much larger gap compared to previous xx80 and xx90 models, where the RTX 4090 only had 68% more cores than the RTX 4080.

Additionally, rumors about power consumption indicate that the RTX 5090 will require up to 600W, pushing the limits of current power supply connectors.

It will be interesting to see what pricing NVIDIA sets for these powerful new GPUs, and how the growing gap between xx80 and xx90 models affects the market.
