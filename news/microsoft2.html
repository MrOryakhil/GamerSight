Microsoft CEO Admits Dropping Windows for Mobile was a Strategic Mistake: Could Windows 12 Bring a Comeback?

Satya Nadella, Microsoft’s CEO, has repeatedly admitted that abandoning the Windows Phone project was a strategic misstep—a sentiment echoed by many fans of the platform. In light of this, a designer has recently unveiled a stunning concept for the Windows 12 Mobile interface, sparking excitement among tech enthusiasts.

Many smartphone critics and experts believe Microsoft should have never made the fateful decision to leave the mobile OS market. If you're among those who wish for a new Windows mobile version, you’re not alone! There are still visionary designers who imagine what Windows 10, 11, and even 12 could look like in the mobile world.

Recently, a well-known concept designer, AR 4789, shared a video on YouTube showcasing what Windows Phone would look like today if it were running Windows 12.

The Windows 12 Mobile Concept is Surprisingly Satisfying

Personally, I found this concept video to be surprisingly satisfying. If a smartphone existed with this operating system and interface, it would no doubt capture the attention of users everywhere. The design takes heavy inspiration from Windows 11 but adds modern features you'd expect in today’s devices, such as an in-display fingerprint sensor and always-on display technology.

AI Everywhere!

Interestingly, this concept includes a sleek taskbar at the bottom of the screen in "Windows 12 Mobile." As expected, apps like Microsoft Copilot, Start Menu, File Explorer, Microsoft Store, and Edge are all neatly packed into it. The design also offers a broad range of customization options, and a handy search bar is placed on the home screen, allowing users to search the web using Microsoft Bing as the default search engine.

Of course, what would a modern interface be without AI? AR 4789 fully understands this and integrated AI-powered tools throughout the Windows 12 Mobile concept. For example, the "Circle to Copilot" feature is a clear nod to Google and Samsung’s "Circle to Search" tool in their latest Galaxy phones.

Could Microsoft Return to the Mobile Market?

As one of the world’s most valuable companies, surpassing Apple, Microsoft has seen its success driven by early investments in generative AI and its partnership with OpenAI. But could Microsoft—with a market value of $3 trillion—learn from past mistakes and re-enter the mobile market with a new Windows Phone that could challenge iOS and Android?

It’s worth noting that Apple has faced significant hurdles with the iPhone 15 series, failing to meet sales expectations, largely due to declining sales in China.

In response, a YouTuber recently captured the imagination of users with a concept for "Windows 12.1," garnering positive feedback for its sleek and functional design. In an 8-minute video, viewers are treated to a beautiful Fluent design for apps, making the concept both visually appealing and practical.

While the future of Windows 12 remains uncertain, many viewers have already urged Microsoft to consider this design for its next mobile operating system.

Watch the Concept Video Now!

This concept showcases a minimal Start Menu, a simplified File Explorer with a clean sidebar for easy navigation, and a taskbar reminiscent of macOS. Additionally, Microsoft Copilot and AI features are integrated, albeit with a lighter touch compared to current Windows versions.
Could this concept inspire Microsoft’s next big move in the mobile world? Only time will tell.
