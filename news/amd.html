AMD Launches EPYC Embedded 8004 Series with Zen 4c Cores

AMD has recently introduced a new generation of EPYC series processors. The EPYC Embedded 8004 processors utilize Zen 4c cores, with core counts ranging from 12 to 64. In this article from City of Hardware, we will explore the specifications of these processors.

Specifications of EPYC Embedded 8004 Processors

The EPYC Embedded 8004 processors are launched in a 1P configuration, supporting up to 1.152 terabytes of DDR5 memory via six channels. The TDP of these processors ranges from 70 to 225 watts, depending on the number of cores.

Zen 4c Microarchitecture in EPYC Embedded 8004 Processors

The Zen 4c microarchitecture is aimed at better performance per watt and is essentially a more compact version of the Zen 4 microarchitecture, focusing on improving energy efficiency at lower clock speeds. This architecture allows AMD to fit more cores in a smaller space.

In comparison, we have the Embedded 9654P processor with 96 cores and a boost clock of 3.7 GHz, with a TDP of 360 watts. In summary, the 8004 series chips have fewer cores, lower frequencies, and generally lower TDP.
