Introduction

God of War Ragnarok comes into the scene as one of the most anticipated releases in recent years. Developed by Santa Monica Studio and published by Sony Interactive Entertainment, this 2022 action-adventure game serves as a direct sequel to the critically acclaimed *God of War* (2018). With its deep narrative, complex combat system, and beautiful visuals, Ragnarok aims to push the boundaries of the series even further. But does it deliver on the high expectations set by its last franchise? In this review, we'll explore the gameplay, story, Graphics and music that define God of War Ragnarok.

Platforms and Release date

God of War Ragnarok was initially released on November 9, 2022, exclusively for the PlayStation 4 and Play Station 5.

However, a PC version is coming out for PC gamers, currently being ported by Jetpack Studios and is expected to be available on September 19, 2024. The PC version is expected to offer enhanced features like higher frame rates, true 4K resolution, and support for Nvidia’s DLSS, AMD’s FSR, and Intel’s XeSS for reaching higher graphical settings. These will allow players to experience the game's stunning visuals and intense combat with even greater fidelity and smoothness.

Also, not to forget to mention that at launch, God of War Ragnarok will include the Valhalla DLC too.

Story and Narrative

God of War: Ragnarok continues the journey of Kratos and his son, Atreus, in the rich and dangerous world of Norse mythology. Picking up after the events of the 2018 game, the story delves deeper into their relationship as they face new challenges and threats. Set against the looming backdrop of Ragnarok, the prophesied end of the world, Kratos and Atreus must navigate the perilous realms, encountering both old allies and formidable foes.

The narrative masterfully builds on the foundation laid by its predecessor, exploring themes of destiny, family, and survival. While it answers some of the questions left open by the previous game, it also introduces new mysteries and conflicts that keep the story engaging and intense, all without giving away any key plot details.

Gameplay

God of War Ragnarok builds on the solid foundation of its predecessor, refining and expanding the gameplay to offer a more dynamic and engaging experience. The core combat system remains as satisfying as ever, with Kratos wielding his iconic Leviathan Axe and Blades of Chaos. However, this time around, the game introduces new mechanics, making battles even more strategic and varied.

Players can now enjoy a wider array of gear and abilities, allowing for more customization in both combat and exploration. The addition of new weapons and armor sets not only enhances Kratos's offensive and defensive capabilities but also opens up different playstyles.

Exploration has also been enriched with more varied environments and challenges, encouraging players to delve deeper into the game's world. The puzzles and traversal mechanics have been improved, offering fresh ways to interact with the environment.

These elements make the gameplay in Ragnarok more enjoyable, keeping it exciting throughout the entire game.

Graphics and Music

God of War Ragnarok is a visual showcase that maximizes the capabilities of both the PlayStation 5 and PlayStation 4. On the PS5, the game delivers breathtaking 4K resolution, detailed textures, and advanced lighting effects like ray tracing, all running at smooth frame rates. The environments are richly detailed, and the seamless transitions between exploration and combat highlight the power of the hardware. Even on the PlayStation 4, Ragnarok remains visually impressive, with careful optimization that ensures a stable and enjoyable experience despite the older hardware.

The upcoming PC port is also going to take these visual achievements even further. Players on PC can look forward to higher frame rates that provide an ultra-smooth experience, particularly during fast-paced combat. The port will also support ultrawide-screen, offering a more expansive view of the game’s stunning environments. Additionally, the PC version will feature customizable graphical settings, allowing players to tweak and enhance the visuals to their liking. From improved shadow details to more complex particle effects, the PC port is aimed to deliver the most visually refined version of Ragnarok yet, making it the ultimate experience for those with high-end gaming rigs.

Last word

God of War Ragnarok stands as a powerful testament to the evolution of one of gaming's most iconic franchises. With its captivating story, refined gameplay mechanics, and impressive use of hardware, it effectively builds on the success of its predecessor while pushing the series into new territory. The game’s breathtaking visuals and intricate sound design are masterfully presented on both the PlayStation 5 and PlayStation 4, with the upcoming PC port promising even more enhancements for those seeking the ultimate experience.
